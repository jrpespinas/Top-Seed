# Aesthetic Feel Reference Notes

## What Works

- Soft off-white or warm gray page backgrounds create a premium, calm canvas.
- Rounded modular cards make dense information feel approachable.
- Deep green and charcoal panels create strong operational focus.
- Warm orange, coral, and yellow accents add energy without needing decorative sports graphics.
- Large numeric/status typography helps quick scanning.
- Subtle borders and shadows separate cards without visual noise.
- Sports imagery feels premium when used outside the core operations surface.
- Iconography is clean and restrained.

## What Fits Top Seed

- A premium sports-club feel for the overall product.
- Deep green court cards for the `Now` state.
- Warm highlighted next queue for staged matches.
- Light player pool cards with compact player-token rows.
- Rounded cards and calm spacing around dense queue/court information.
- Charcoal panels only for high-emphasis summaries, not every card.

## What Does Not Fit

- Marketing-heavy image panels inside the live session dashboard.
- Decorative charts that do not help the organizer run games.
- Fitness/wellness metrics that make the queueing app feel unrelated to badminton operations.
- Overly glossy visuals that reduce readability in a bright sports hall.
- Mobile booking-app patterns for the organizer-only MVP.

## Borrow

- Mood: premium sports/wellness, calm but energetic.
- Surfaces: warm neutral canvas with rounded cards.
- Contrast: deep green/charcoal for operational focus.
- Accent: warm orange/yellow for next queue, active state, and attention.
- Rhythm: modular dashboard cards with generous breathing room.
- Typography: large numbers/status only where they support quick decisions.

## Do Not Copy

- Exact layouts, brand marks, imagery, colors, or typography from references.
- Decorative athlete photos in the live pegboard area.
- Generic analytics/dashboard widgets.
- Visual effects that hurt contrast or touch clarity.
- Any pattern that competes with courts, next queue, or player pool.

## Visual Translation For MVP

```text
Warm neutral app shell
Left player pool: light cards + compact player tokens
Center courts: deep green court containers + white court lines/team slots
Right next queue: warm accent staging lane + queued match cards
Support panels: calm white cards for payments, history, leaderboard
```

The final product should feel custom, premium, and sports-specific while still behaving like a practical badminton pegboard.
